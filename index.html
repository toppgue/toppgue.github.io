About me

From complexity
to clarity.

My background is in environmental engineering — a field that taught me to think in systems, look for long-term impact, and never lose sight of what actually matters. Those instincts carried through into everything I've done since: complex international projects, technology-driven business environments, and a constant search for sustainable, meaningful solutions.

But at some point, I turned that same analytical curiosity inward. I became genuinely passionate about personal growth — not as a trend, but as a practice. I started exploring tools and frameworks that helped me understand myself better, identify where I was thriving, and face honestly where I wasn't.

That shift was a real level up. Not a dramatic overnight change, but a steady, compounding one — the kind that builds quietly and then, one day, you look back and realize how far you've come.

What I've learned is that growth rarely requires a perfect plan. More often, it requires curiosity — the willingness to ask the next question, try the next thing, and trust that momentum, even without a clear destination, will point you toward your most authentic direction.

This page is a small corner of the internet where I share the things that made a difference for me — tools, reflections, and maybe soon some of the trails I've walked. Starting with the exercise that started it all.

Why I share this
I don't share tools I haven't used myself. The Circle of Life below is one I've returned to again and again — at different moments, in different seasons of my life. It gave me snapshots. It helped me focus on one area at a time. And it showed me something I didn't expect: that improving one area of your life tends to lift others too. A quiet domino effect.
